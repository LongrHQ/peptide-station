# Metabolic & GLP-1 Peptides

> **Category file:** Tirzepatide · Semaglutide · Adipotide
>
> This category covers the most clinically impactful weight management peptides available. Tirzepatide and Semaglutide are FDA-approved pharmaceuticals with the strongest evidence base in this entire catalogue. Adipotide is an experimental, mechanism-divergent fat-targeting compound at very early research stage. Evidence quality: Tirzepatide and Semaglutide have gold-standard Phase III RCTs; Adipotide is animal/early phase only.

---

## Tirzepatide

**Category:** Metabolic / GLP-1 + GIP Dual Agonist / Anti-obesity pharmaceutical
**Also known as:** Mounjaro® (T2DM), Zepbound® (obesity), LY3298176, "twincretin"

### Overview & Mechanism

Tirzepatide is a synthetic 39-amino-acid peptide that functions as a **dual agonist of both the GLP-1 (glucagon-like peptide-1) receptor and the GIP (glucose-dependent insulinotropic polypeptide) receptor** — sometimes called a "twincretin" because it mimics both incretin hormones simultaneously. It is based on the native GIP sequence with modifications for extended half-life (~5 days), enabling once-weekly subcutaneous dosing. The dual mechanism distinguishes it from pure GLP-1 agonists (like semaglutide): GIP receptor activation may amplify weight loss through additional CNS pathways, enhancing feelings of fullness and possibly increasing energy expenditure, while GLP-1 activation drives glucose-dependent insulin secretion, glucagon suppression, gastric emptying delay, and CNS satiety signalling.

FDA approved tirzepatide under the brand Mounjaro® for Type 2 diabetes (May 2022) and Zepbound® for chronic weight management in obesity (November 2023). Clinical data from the SURMOUNT trials represent landmark achievements in obesity pharmacotherapy:
- **SURMOUNT-1** (72 weeks, n=2539): Up to **22.5% mean body weight loss** with 15 mg tirzepatide — the highest ever recorded in a Phase III obesity drug trial at the time
- **SURMOUNT-4**: Weight regain prevention — tirzepatide maintained weight loss vs placebo over continued treatment
- **SURMOUNT-5** (2024): Head-to-head vs semaglutide 2.4 mg — tirzepatide produced **47% more weight loss** (20.2% vs 13.7% at 72 weeks). Women on tirzepatide lost 23.8% vs 18.0% on semaglutide; men 17.8% vs 11.0%.
- **SURPASS-CVOT**: Cardiovascular outcomes trial confirmed significant MACE reduction in patients with T2DM and established CVD.

### Primary Use Cases

- **Obesity treatment:** FDA-approved for BMI ≥30, or ≥27 with weight-related comorbidity
- **Type 2 diabetes:** FDA-approved; superior glycaemic control vs most comparators
- **Cardiovascular risk reduction:** Confirmed MACE reduction in T2DM with CVD
- **Non-alcoholic fatty liver disease (NAFLD/MASH):** Strong emerging evidence; Phase III SURPASS-NASH data positive
- **Pre-diabetes / metabolic syndrome:** Off-label but widely used to prevent T2DM progression
- **Biohacker / longevity weight optimisation:** Used as the GLP-1 agent of choice for maximum fat loss with body composition goals

### Evidence Quality

**Highest evidence tier — multiple Phase III RCTs, FDA approved, cardiovascular outcomes data.**

Tirzepatide has the strongest clinical evidence base of any compound in this catalogue. SURMOUNT and SURPASS trial programmes represent the largest body of obesity/diabetes trial data for any single compound. Independent replication is inherent in the multi-national RCT designs (JAMA, NEJM, Lancet publications). Cardiovascular safety confirmed. The only significant evidence caveat is the relative novelty — long-term (10+ year) safety data is still accumulating. Evidence strength: **Gold standard. Phase III RCT evidence at highest level.**

### Quiz Signals

- ✅ BMI ≥30 or ≥27 with a **weight-related comorbidity** (T2DM, hypertension, sleep apnoea, dyslipidaemia) — meets approved criteria
- ✅ Tried other weight loss approaches (diet, exercise, other medications) without **sustained 10%+ body weight reduction**
- ✅ Has **Type 2 diabetes** and desires a single agent addressing both glycaemic control and weight
- ✅ **Maximum fat loss is the priority** — data shows tirzepatide outperforms all other injectable GLP-1 options including semaglutide
- ✅ Has **cardiovascular risk factors** or established CVD where MACE risk reduction is clinically meaningful
- ✅ Seeking the **most extensively evidenced weight loss peptide** available

### Risks & Regulatory Notes

- **Nausea, vomiting, diarrhoea, constipation:** Most common adverse effects, especially during dose escalation; occur in 10–40% of patients, usually mild-moderate and transient
- **Thyroid C-cell tumours:** Black box warning — based on rodent carcinogenicity studies; avoid in patients with personal or family history of medullary thyroid carcinoma (MTC) or MEN2 syndrome
- **Pancreatitis:** Rare but reported; contraindicated in history of pancreatitis
- **Gallbladder disease:** Increased risk of cholelithiasis (gallstones) with rapid weight loss
- **Hypoglycaemia:** Low risk as monotherapy; significant risk when combined with insulin or sulfonylureas
- **Gastroparesis:** Gastric emptying delay can impair oral medication absorption
- **Muscle loss:** Rapid weight loss may include lean mass; resistance training and adequate protein intake recommended
- **Contraindications:** Pregnancy, personal/family history MTC or MEN2, history of pancreatitis
- **FDA status:** Approved (Mounjaro® for T2DM, Zepbound® for obesity)
- **WADA:** Not currently Prohibited; semaglutide placed on WADA monitoring programme in 2024 (GLP-1 class monitoring). Tirzepatide's WADA status should be checked annually — class-level monitoring may extend to tirzepatide.
- **Compounding:** As of 2024–2025, compounded tirzepatide in the US was legal during FDA-declared shortage; regulatory status evolving — check current FDA guidance

### Education Hook Facts

1. **In the SURMOUNT-1 trial, tirzepatide produced 22.5% mean body weight loss over 72 weeks** — equivalent to losing 25+ kg for the average obese adult. No previously approved weight loss drug came close.
2. **Head-to-head against semaglutide (SURMOUNT-5, 2024), tirzepatide produced 47% more weight loss** — a dramatic difference suggesting the dual GIP/GLP-1 mechanism genuinely outperforms GLP-1 alone.
3. **Women responded better than men** in SURMOUNT-5 (23.8% vs 18% weight loss for women on tirzepatide) — an unexpected reversal of the usual pattern where men lose more weight on conventional diets.

### Dosing Reference

**FDA-approved titration schedule (Zepbound® / Mounjaro®):**

| Week | Dose |
|------|------|
| 0–3 | 2.5 mg once weekly |
| 4–7 | 5 mg once weekly |
| 8–11 | 7.5 mg once weekly (if needed) |
| 12–15 | 10 mg once weekly (if needed) |
| 16–19 | 12.5 mg once weekly (if needed) |
| 20+ | 15 mg once weekly (maximum) |

- **Maintenance dose:** 5–15 mg once weekly (titrate to tolerability and response)
- **Route:** Subcutaneous injection (abdomen, thigh, upper arm)
- **Interval:** Once weekly, same day each week; if missed >4 days, skip to next scheduled dose

## Semaglutide

**Category:** Metabolic / GLP-1 Receptor Agonist / Anti-obesity pharmaceutical
**Also known as:** Ozempic® (T2DM injection), Wegovy® (obesity injection), Rybelsus® (T2DM oral), GLP-1RA

### Overview & Mechanism

Semaglutide is a synthetic GLP-1 receptor agonist (GLP-1RA) — a 94% sequence homology analogue of native GLP-1 with fatty acid and linker modifications that extend its half-life to approximately **8 days** (enabling once-weekly injection or once-daily oral dosing). Developed by Novo Nordisk, semaglutide activates the GLP-1 receptor in multiple tissues: in the pancreas it drives **glucose-dependent insulin secretion** (safe because insulin release only occurs when glucose is elevated) and suppresses glucagon; in the gut it slows gastric emptying; in the hypothalamus and brainstem it activates satiety centres and reduces appetite via direct CNS receptor binding.

The weight loss mechanism is primarily **appetite suppression and reduced caloric intake** rather than metabolic rate change — patients on semaglutide consistently report reduced hunger, food preoccupation, and smaller portion satisfaction. Secondary mechanisms include improved insulin sensitivity and possible direct fat cell metabolism effects. Key clinical programmes:
- **STEP trials (Wegovy® 2.4 mg):** STEP-1 showed **14.9% mean body weight loss** over 68 weeks vs 2.4% placebo. Patients who stopped treatment regained most of the weight.
- **SELECT trial (2023, NEJM):** In 17,604 overweight/obese patients with established CVD (no diabetes), semaglutide 2.4 mg reduced **MACE risk by 20%** — a landmark finding extending benefit beyond diabetic populations.
- **SUSTAIN trials (Ozempic® 0.5–2 mg):** Robust HbA1c reduction and MACE risk reduction in T2DM.

### Primary Use Cases

- **Obesity treatment:** FDA-approved Wegovy® for BMI ≥30 or ≥27 with comorbidity
- **Type 2 diabetes:** FDA-approved Ozempic® for glycaemic control and cardiovascular risk reduction
- **Cardiovascular risk reduction:** SELECT trial evidence for MACE reduction even without diabetes
- **NAFLD/metabolic-associated steatotic liver disease:** Positive Phase III evidence
- **Pre-diabetes / metabolic syndrome:** Off-label use to prevent T2DM progression
- **Addiction and food reward:** Emerging research on GLP-1's role in reducing alcohol, food, and addictive cravings (active research area, not yet approved indication)

### Evidence Quality

**Highest evidence tier — multiple Phase III RCTs across diabetes and obesity indications, FDA approved, cardiovascular outcomes landmark data (SELECT).**

Semaglutide has the most comprehensive evidence base of any GLP-1RA. SELECT trial (NEJM 2023) is one of the largest and most impactful obesity treatment trials ever conducted. Independent replication across STEP and SUSTAIN programmes, published in top-tier journals. International regulatory approvals across FDA, EMA, MHRA. Long-term safety is more established than tirzepatide given longer market presence. Key limitation: weight regain after discontinuation is well-documented. Evidence strength: **Gold standard across all indications — among the most evidence-rich compounds in modern medicine.**

### Quiz Signals

- ✅ BMI ≥30 or ≥27 with **weight-related comorbidity** — meets approved Wegovy® criteria
- ✅ Has **Type 2 diabetes** and needs both glycaemic control and weight management
- ✅ **Established cardiovascular disease** (prior MI, stroke, or peripheral arterial disease) with overweight/obesity — SELECT trial directly supports this use
- ✅ Has tried tirzepatide but experienced **intolerability** or cost barrier — semaglutide is more established with longer safety track record
- ✅ Wants a **once-weekly injection** with strong clinical evidence and full regulatory backing
- ✅ Managing **food addiction, emotional eating, or alcohol use** — emerging GLP-1 CNS effects research relevant

### Risks & Regulatory Notes

- **Nausea, vomiting, diarrhoea, constipation:** Common, especially during titration; typically transient; nausea in ~44% of patients vs ~16% placebo in STEP trials
- **Thyroid C-cell tumours:** Black box warning (same class as tirzepatide — rodent data); contraindicated with personal/family history of MTC or MEN2
- **Pancreatitis:** Rare but reported
- **Gallbladder disease:** Increased cholelithiasis risk with rapid weight loss; higher absolute rate in SELECT trial vs comparators
- **Weight regain on discontinuation:** STEP-4 extension showed ~14% weight regain within 1 year of stopping — dependency of maintained effect on continued use
- **Muscle mass loss:** As with all rapid weight loss — resistance exercise and protein intake important
- **Gastroparesis:** Risk, especially with pre-existing gastric motility issues; concerns about emergency anaesthesia in patients with slowed gastric emptying
- **Drug interactions:** Slowed gastric emptying affects oral medication absorption timing
- **Contraindications:** Pregnancy, personal/family history MTC or MEN2, pancreatitis history
- **FDA status:** Approved — Ozempic® (T2DM), Wegovy® (obesity), Rybelsus® (T2DM oral)
- **WADA:** Added to WADA Monitoring Programme in 2024 (not Prohibited, but monitored for prevalence and potential performance enhancement effects). Reviewed annually — could be added to Prohibited List in future.
- **NHS/prescribing access:** Significant rationing in the UK due to cost and global supply constraints; BMI and comorbidity thresholds strictly applied in NHS pathway

### Education Hook Facts

1. **The SELECT trial showed semaglutide reduced cardiovascular death, heart attack, and stroke by 20%** in overweight/obese people who had already had a cardiovascular event — even without diabetes. This reframes GLP-1 as a cardiovascular drug, not just a weight loss drug.
2. **STEP-1 participants who stopped semaglutide regained ~14% of their body weight within one year** — confirming that semaglutide treats obesity as an ongoing condition requiring continuous treatment, not a cure.
3. **Early data from multiple studies suggests GLP-1 receptor agonists may reduce alcohol craving, smoking desire, and other addictive behaviours** — opening an entirely new therapeutic frontier beyond metabolic disease.

### Dosing Reference

**FDA-approved Wegovy® titration schedule (obesity indication):**

| Weeks | Dose |
|-------|------|
| 1–4 | 0.25 mg once weekly |
| 5–8 | 0.5 mg once weekly |
| 9–12 | 1.0 mg once weekly |
| 13–16 | 1.7 mg once weekly |
| 17+ | 2.4 mg once weekly (maintenance) |

- **Maintenance dose:** 2.4 mg once weekly (recommended) or 1.7 mg if not tolerated
- **Route:** Subcutaneous injection (abdomen, thigh, upper arm)
- **Ozempic® (T2DM):** Start 0.25 mg × 4 weeks → 0.5 mg → 1 mg → 2 mg (titration every 4 weeks)
- **Rybelsus® (oral):** 3 mg once daily × 30 days → 7 mg → 14 mg; taken fasting with small sip of water

## Adipotide

**Category:** Metabolic / Experimental fat-targeting peptide
**Also known as:** FTPP (Fat-Targeted Proapoptotic Peptide), Prohibitin-TP01 (TP01), GKGGRAKDC-GG-D(KLAKLAK)2

### Overview & Mechanism

Adipotide is a chimeric synthetic peptidomimetic — a bispecific molecule engineered to do two things simultaneously: (1) **target the vasculature of white adipose tissue** by binding to the prohibitin-1 (PHB1) / Annexin A2 (ANXA2) receptor complex expressed on the endothelial cells supplying white fat depots, and (2) **induce apoptosis** in those same cells via a KLAKLAK proapoptotic domain. By selectively destroying the blood supply to white adipose tissue (particularly visceral and subcutaneous fat), it causes adipocyte ischaemia and subsequent death — the dead adipose cells are cleared by the immune system.

This mechanism is radically different from all other fat-loss approaches: it is not hormonal (no GH, no GLP-1), not metabolic (no AMPK, no lipolysis), but directly **structural** — eliminating the vasculature that keeps fat cells alive. Animal studies conducted at MD Anderson Cancer Center (Barnhart et al., PNAS) showed dramatic fat loss in mice (>30% body fat reduction) and, remarkably, in obese Rhesus macaques (~39% reduction in visceral fat, improved insulin sensitivity within weeks). The Rhesus macaque study is notable because non-human primates share PHB1/ANXA2 expression patterns much closer to humans than rodents.

### Primary Use Cases

- **Research model for studying adipose vasculature:** Currently the most meaningful "real-world" application — used as a scientific tool
- **Experimental fat loss (biohacker research):** Community interest despite very limited human safety data
- **Visceral fat reduction:** Animal data suggests visceral fat is particularly targeted
- **Metabolic research:** Understanding the role of fat vasculature in insulin resistance and metabolic disease
- **Potential cancer research:** PHB1-targeting is also investigated in oncology contexts given PHB1's role in tumour vasculature

### Evidence Quality

**Preclinical only (mice + non-human primate). No published human clinical trial data.**

Mechanistic and animal evidence is compelling — particularly the Rhesus macaque data (Barnhart et al., 2004; Science). This is significantly more translatable than rodent-only data. However, **no human clinical trials have been published or registered**. The proapoptotic mechanism (killing vascular cells) raises significant safety concerns that have prevented clinical progression. The research compound market for Adipotide is driven primarily by the dramatic animal results, not human evidence. Evidence strength: **Intriguing animal evidence; no human safety or efficacy data. Approach with high caution.**

### Quiz Signals

- ✅ Has **severe, treatment-resistant visceral obesity** and is researching every possible mechanism available
- ✅ Specifically interested in a peptide that targets the **biological structure of fat tissue**, not just metabolism or appetite
- ✅ Has **already tried GLP-1 medications, AOD-9604, and lifestyle interventions** without satisfactory results
- ✅ Is a **researcher or advanced biohacker** with a strong understanding of risk-benefit and willingness to engage with animal-only evidence
- ✅ Focused on **visceral fat specifically** (apple-body phenotype, metabolic syndrome) as the primary target
- ✅ Willing to accept **very high uncertainty** around safety — the proapoptotic mechanism is inherently aggressive

### Risks & Regulatory Notes

- **Nephrotoxicity:** Observed in Rhesus macaque studies — kidney function impairment was a significant adverse finding; represents a major safety concern
- **Proapoptotic mechanism:** Killing endothelial cells is inherently non-selective — off-target apoptosis in non-adipose vasculature is a theoretical risk
- **No human safety data:** Cannot extrapolate safe dose or frequency without clinical trials
- **PHB1 expression not exclusive to fat vasculature:** PHB1 is expressed in other tissues — the degree of selectivity in humans is unknown
- **Immune system reactions:** Clearing large amounts of dead adipose tissue could trigger systemic inflammatory responses
- **Not FDA approved; no GRAS status**
- **WADA:** Not listed; very unlikely to be used in competitive sports context
- **Regulatory:** Research compound only; no pathway toward clinical approval currently active
- **Rapid weight loss risks:** Even if effective, very rapid fat loss creates metabolic perturbations, electrolyte risks, and potential liver stress

### Education Hook Facts

1. **In obese Rhesus macaques, Adipotide reduced visceral fat by ~39% and improved insulin sensitivity significantly** — results so dramatic that researchers called it "unlike anything we've seen with other experimental obesity treatments."
2. **Adipotide works by destroying the blood supply to white fat cells** — a completely different approach to fat loss than any appetite drug or metabolic agent. It's more like targeted surgery than pharmacology.
3. **PHB1 (prohibitin), the receptor Adipotide targets, is selectively expressed on the endothelial cells feeding white adipose tissue** — a molecular address system that makes targeted fat vascular ablation theoretically possible.

### Dosing Reference

**Animal research reference (no human protocol exists):**
- **Mice:** ~2.5 mg/kg/day subcutaneous injection × 28 days
- **Rhesus macaque:** Lower doses — exact regimen not fully published

*There is no established human dosing protocol. The nephrotoxicity observed in primate studies means any human use would require careful renal function monitoring. This compound should not be self-administered without clinical oversight and baseline/ongoing kidney function testing.*
