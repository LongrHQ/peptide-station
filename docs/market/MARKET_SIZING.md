# Peptide Station — Market Sizing

---

## Summary

The peptide market is large, multi-layered, and growing. The clinical pharmaceutical peptide market is $46–141B globally (wide range due to different analyst scopes). The consumer/biohacker peptide market is a rapidly growing subset that is very early-stage and extremely underserved. Peptide Station targets the consumer end.

## 2. Consumer Peptide Market

The clinical/pharmaceutical market (insulin, semaglutide, etc.) is the majority of the $46–141B figure. The **consumer/grey-market/research chemical peptide market** is a distinct, much smaller, but rapidly growing sub-segment.

### Sizing the Consumer Market

There is no clean public data for the grey-market/research chemical peptide consumer market. We can triangulate:

**Method 1: Search demand proxy**
- "BPC 157" alone: 301,000 searches/month in the US = ~3.6M/year
- Including sermorelin (165K/month), ipamorelin (40K), GHK-Cu (110K), etc.: total addressable US query volume ~700K–1M/month for consumer peptide intent
- If 2% convert to purchase at $60 AOV = ~$1B/year US consumer market (very rough)

**Method 2: Market extrapolation**
- Grand View Research estimates global biohacking market at $24.81B in 2024 (growing 19% CAGR to $69B by 2030)
- Injectable/peptide products are a meaningful subset of biohacking spend
- Conservative estimate: peptide consumer segment = $500M–2B globally in 2024

**Method 3: Vendor revenues**
- Hims GLP-1 (clinical) generated $200M+ in 2024 — demonstrates consumer willingness to spend
- Research chemical vendors are private; Limitless Biotech, SwissChems etc. not publicly reported
- Direct Peptides (UK) appears to list 52+ pen products — established commercial vendor

**Best estimate for consumer peptide market:** $500M–1.5B globally in 2025, growing at 20%+ CAGR (faster than clinical market due to low base and cultural tailwinds).

## 3. US Market (Primary Target)

### US Peptide Market Context

The United States is the world's largest consumer peptide market by search volume and spend. It is also the most culturally advanced market for biohacking, longevity, and self-injectable health products (normalised by the GLP-1/Ozempic revolution).

- **US health supplement market:** $50B+ annually (largest globally)
- **US biohacking market:** Estimated $8–12B in 2025 (fastest-growing segment of wellness)
- **US telehealth market:** $62B in 2025 — consumer comfort with digital health purchases is high
- **US compounding pharmacy market:** $14B+ in 2025 — demonstrates demand for personalised pharmaceutical products

**Search volume dominance (US vs all other markets):**
- BPC 157: 301,000/month US
- GHK-Cu: 110,000/month US
- Sermorelin: 165,000/month US
- "Peptide therapy near me": 6,600–8,100/month US (growing strongly)
- "Research peptides": 9,900–14,800/month US (3x growth in 12 months)

### US-Specific Growth Drivers

1. **Telehealth boom:** US telehealth normalised self-managed health products. Hims & Hers built $200M+ GLP-1 business; consumers are comfortable injecting at home.
2. **GLP-1 craze:** Ozempic/Wegovy made peptide self-injection culturally mainstream. Adjacent peptides (Sermorelin, BPC-157) benefit from halo effect.
3. **Compounding pharmacy market:** $14B+ market demonstrates appetite for personalised pharmaceutical-grade products. Peptide consumers see compounded peptides as a natural extension.
4. **MAHA movement:** RFK Jr. allies (Gary Brecka, Dr. Mark Hyman) publicly advocate peptides, driving mainstream awareness. RFK Jr. has expressed intent to reduce FDA's "war on peptides."
5. **Influencer velocity:** US has the highest density of longevity/biohacking influencers globally (Huberman Lab, Gary Brecka, Ben Greenfield, Jay Campbell, Brian Johnson).
6. **Media saturation:** NYT (Jan 2026), CNN (Nov 2025), ABC News (Nov 2025), Marketplace all covered US peptide trend. Cultural moment is at peak.

### US Regulatory Challenge

The FDA has been significantly more aggressive than the UK's MHRA:
- **BPC-157:** FDA Category 2 — prohibited for compounding by licensed pharmacies
- **September 2025:** FDA issued 50+ warning letters to peptide vendors in a single month
- **FDA warning letter doctrine:** "Research use only" framing does NOT provide cover if products are clearly marketed for human administration
- **State variation:** Ohio Board of Pharmacy issued suspension orders; other states may follow

**This creates a paradox:** The largest, most lucrative market has the most aggressive enforcement. US strategy requires careful research-use-only framing, specialist regulatory counsel, and avoidance of any explicit human-use claims.

**US addressable consumer peptide market estimate:** $500M–1.5B in 2025, growing at 25%+ CAGR

## 7. Key Market Sizing Caveats

1. **Grey market opacity:** No official sales data; all consumer estimates are extrapolations
2. **Regulatory overhang:** FDA enforcement is active and accelerating (50+ warning letters Sep 2025); could contract US market if enforcement escalates to seizures/injunctions
3. **Clinical market inclusion:** Many "peptide market" figures include insulin, GLP-1 drugs — inflating vs. consumer reality
4. **US consumer data:** No clean public data on the grey-market/research chemical consumer segment; triangulation required
5. **Trend vs. enduring:** Some concern peptide consumer trend is media-driven hype; underlying research quality for most compounds (BPC-157, GHK-Cu, Sermorelin) suggests enduring demand
6. **MAHA tailwind uncertainty:** RFK Jr. / MAHA policy could either open or close the US market depending on whether HHS directs FDA to reduce or increase enforcement
