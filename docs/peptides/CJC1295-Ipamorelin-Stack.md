# CJC-1295 + Ipamorelin Stack

**Category:** Growth Hormone Support (GHRH Analogue + GHRP / Ghrelin Mimetic)
**Also known as:** CJC/Ipam, Mod GRF(1-29) + Ipamorelin, GH Peptide Stack
**Source:** RBT Supplier Catalogue + Tavily Research
**Last updated:** 2026-04-09

---

## Overview

The CJC-1295 + Ipamorelin stack is the most widely prescribed peptide combination in anti-aging and longevity medicine. It pairs two complementary peptides that stimulate growth hormone (GH) release through entirely different receptor pathways — creating a synergistic effect that neither peptide achieves alone.

**CJC-1295** (used without DAC in most clinical protocols) is a stabilised synthetic analogue of GHRH(1-29), the same 29-amino-acid fragment that makes sermorelin effective — but with chemical modifications that extend its half-life from ~7 minutes to approximately 30 minutes. This makes it meaningfully longer-acting than sermorelin while still producing physiologically pulsatile GH release.

> **Naming note:** In clinical settings, "CJC-1295" often refers to the **without-DAC** version (also called Mod GRF 1-29). CJC-1295 **with DAC** (Drug Affinity Complex) has a dramatically extended half-life of 6–8 days, producing a blunted, sustained GH elevation rather than natural pulses. The without-DAC version is more commonly stacked with ipamorelin for its pulsatile profile. This document covers both, noting where they differ.

**Ipamorelin** is a synthetic pentapeptide and selective ghrelin receptor (GHS-R1a) agonist — the first truly "clean" growth hormone releasing peptide (GHRP). Unlike earlier GHRPs (GHRP-2, GHRP-6), ipamorelin does not trigger meaningful cortisol, prolactin, or appetite increases, making it the preferred GHRP for most clinical GH optimisation protocols.

Together, they represent the "gold standard" peptide combination for growth hormone optimisation in the anti-aging and longevity space.

## CJC-1295: Mechanism of Action

CJC-1295 (without DAC) acts as a **GHRH receptor agonist** on anterior pituitary somatotrophs. It follows the same pathway as sermorelin:

1. **GHRHR binding** → Gs protein activation → adenylate cyclase → ↑ cAMP
2. **Stimulates GH gene transcription** and increases pituitary GH reserve
3. **Triggers pulsatile GH release** — physiologically natural pattern
4. **IGF-1 upregulation** via hepatic GH signalling

**CJC-1295 vs. Sermorelin:**
- Both are GHRH analogues derived from the same 1-29 fragment
- CJC-1295 has chemical modifications (4 amino acid substitutions at positions 2, 8, 15, and 27) that resist enzymatic degradation and extend half-life to ~30 min vs sermorelin's ~10–20 min
- The longer half-life produces a stronger, more sustained GH pulse per dose
- CJC-1295 elevates IGF-1 more significantly than sermorelin

**CJC-1295 with DAC:**
- The DAC (Drug Affinity Complex) modification allows covalent binding to serum albumin, extending half-life to 6–8 days
- This produces a sustained "GH bleed" rather than natural pulses
- Published clinical data: Teichman et al. (2006, PubMed PMID 16352683) showed mean IGF-1 levels remained above baseline for up to 28 days after multiple CJC-1295 doses, with an estimated half-life of 5.8–8.1 days
- ⚠️ One unpublished trial using CJC-1295 DAC in HIV lipodystrophy was terminated early after a patient death; causality was not established and data were not published. FDA noted this in its December 2024 PCAC briefing.
- Most clinicians now prefer CJC-1295 without DAC for pulsatile GH profiles, stacked with ipamorelin

## Ipamorelin: Mechanism of Action

Ipamorelin is a **pentapeptide (Aib-His-D-2-Nal-D-Phe-Lys-NH2)** that selectively binds the **ghrelin receptor (GHS-R1a)** on pituitary somatotrophs and hypothalamic neurons. Its mechanism is entirely distinct from GHRH analogues:

1. **GHS-R1a activation** → intracellular Ca²⁺ mobilisation → GH secretion
2. **Somatostatin inhibition** — reduces the "brake" on GH release at the hypothalamus, amplifying the GH pulse that CJC-1295 triggers
3. **Selective GH release** — unlike GHRP-2 and GHRP-6, ipamorelin does not meaningfully stimulate cortisol, prolactin, ACTH, aldosterone, or appetite

**Selectivity is ipamorelin's defining feature.** Early GHRPs (GHRP-6 in particular) caused significant appetite stimulation and cortisol spikes — side effects that limited their clinical utility. Ipamorelin was specifically developed to avoid these off-target effects, making it the preferred GHRP since its characterisation in 1998 (Raun et al., *European Journal of Endocrinology*).

**Additional research applications:** Ipamorelin has been studied (in rodent models) for gastrointestinal motility — specifically as a potential treatment for postoperative ileus. This is one of the medical indications reviewed by the FDA PCAC in October 2024. Animal data are promising but human trials have not been completed.

## The Stack: Why They're Almost Always Combined

CJC-1295 and ipamorelin target **two different receptor systems** that converge on GH release through complementary mechanisms:

| Peptide | Receptor | Primary Effect |
|---|---|---|
| CJC-1295 | GHRH receptor (GHRHR) | Stimulates somatotrophs to produce and release GH |
| Ipamorelin | Ghrelin receptor (GHS-R1a) | Amplifies GH pulse + suppresses somatostatin inhibition |

**Why this matters:** GH release is regulated by a push-pull balance between GHRH (stimulatory) and somatostatin (inhibitory). GHRH analogues like CJC-1295 only work on the stimulatory side. Ipamorelin suppresses somatostatin simultaneously — which means:

> **The stack removes the brake while pressing the accelerator.**

The result is a significantly larger GH pulse than either peptide produces alone. Research and clinical observation consistently show the combination produces **synergistically** elevated GH, not merely additive effects.

**Why CJC-1295 rather than sermorelin in this stack?**
- CJC-1295's longer half-life (30 min vs ~10 min) better matches ipamorelin's duration of action
- This produces a more sustained co-stimulation window per injection
- Sermorelin + ipamorelin does work, but the CJC-1295 combination produces a stronger and more reliable response
- Sermorelin is often the entry point for more cautious or older patients; CJC-1295 + ipamorelin is the step-up

## Synergy Mechanism (Detailed)

When administered together (or in a pre-mixed combination vial), the sequence works as follows:

1. **CJC-1295** binds GHRHR → activates somatotrophs → GH secretion initiated
2. **Ipamorelin** simultaneously binds GHS-R1a → suppresses somatostatin release from the hypothalamus → the normal "brake" on the GH pulse is reduced
3. The resulting GH pulse is **amplified in amplitude** (ipamorelin contribution) and **extended in duration** (CJC-1295 contribution)
4. The combined pulse triggers robust **IGF-1 production** from the liver over the next 12–24 hours
5. GH and IGF-1 then drive downstream effects: lipolysis (fat mobilisation), protein synthesis (muscle), collagen synthesis (connective tissue, skin), and cell repair

**The result:** Natural-feeling, physiologically pulsatile GH elevation — not the flat, continuous elevation of exogenous HGH — with the body's feedback mechanisms still intact.

## Evidence Quality

**Established for CJC-1295:**
- GH and IGF-1 elevation in healthy adults — demonstrated in clinical studies (Teichman et al., 2006; PMID 16352683)
- Prolonged IGF-1 elevation with DAC version — confirmed in a clinical dose-escalation study
- GHRH receptor pharmacology — well-characterised

**Established for Ipamorelin:**
- Selective GH release without cortisol/prolactin elevation — demonstrated in rat studies (Raun et al., 1998; *Eur J Endocrinol*)
- FDA Briefing (PCAC, Oct 2024) acknowledges "ipamorelin, the first selective growth hormone secretagogue" and reviews available human-relevant data
- Postoperative ileus animal models — positive results in rodents (PubMed: 19289567), not yet replicated in human trials

**Moderate / extrapolated for the combination:**
- Body composition improvements — no RCTs directly measuring body composition changes with this specific combination; evidence is inferred from:
  - GH therapy studies in adults (Rudman et al., 1990 NEJM — HGH in men over 60)
  - GH effects on muscle strength (2013 trial showing improved muscle strength after 6 months of GH therapy in men over 50)
  - Journal of Molecular Endocrinology 2014 paper concluding GH "exerts complex multi-system effects on skeletal muscle function" to increase muscle mass
- Anti-aging effects — biologically plausible from GH/IGF-1 biology; no sermorelin-equivalent head-to-head trial for this stack

**Weak / not yet studied:**
- CJC-1295 + ipamorelin combination in a controlled clinical trial — no published RCT exists specifically for this combination
- Long-term safety data (> 6 months) for sustained use — limited

> ⚠️ **Evidence caveat:** The combination is widely used in clinical practice with a strong safety track record (no serious adverse events directly attributable per FDA FAERS review), but robust combination-specific clinical trial data is absent. Benefits are credible based on mechanism and individual component data, but this should be communicated clearly to users.

## Risks & Contraindications

**CJC-1295 — common side effects:**
- Injection site reactions (redness, swelling, pain)
- Flushing, warmth or tingling after injection
- Water retention / mild oedema (GH-mediated)
- Fatigue or drowsiness if dosed during the day (take at night)
- Headache

**Ipamorelin — common side effects:**
- Mild headache post-injection
- Transient flushing
- Dizziness — more common at higher doses
- Mild water retention
- Nausea (uncommon; less frequent than other GHRPs)

**Stack-specific considerations:**
- The amplified GH pulse means insulin resistance risk is higher than either peptide alone — monitor blood glucose in diabetics or pre-diabetics
- IGF-1 elevation is more pronounced with this stack than sermorelin — stay within physiological ranges; confirm with labs

**Contraindications (both peptides):**
- **Active or suspected malignancy** — IGF-1 is mitogenic; growth-promoting signals contraindicated in cancer
- **Intracranial lesions, pituitary tumours** — stimulating the pituitary in these conditions is dangerous
- **Pregnancy and breastfeeding** — insufficient safety data
- **Untreated hypothyroidism** — blunts GH response; optimise thyroid before starting
- **Diabetes / insulin resistance** — GH is counter-regulatory to insulin; requires monitoring and possible medication adjustment
- **Already elevated IGF-1 or GH** — adding further stimulation risks acromegaly-like effects
- **CJC-1295 with DAC specifically:** One early-stage human trial was terminated following a patient death (cause unconfirmed, data unpublished) — the FDA cited this in its December 2024 PCAC review

**Drug interactions:**
- Glucocorticoids — attenuate GH response
- Insulin / antidiabetic medications — glucose regulation may need adjustment
- Other peptides with overlapping mechanisms — monitor IGF-1 to avoid excessive elevation
- TRT / anabolic hormones — generally compatible and synergistic, but medical supervision required

**Regulatory status:**
- **CJC-1295 (all forms):** Never FDA-approved. Reviewed by the FDA Pharmacy Compounding Advisory Committee (PCAC) in **December 2024** — FDA expressed concerns about evidence gaps and safety data for compounding. As of early 2026, regulatory clarity for compounded CJC-1295 is evolving; the compounding pathway remains in flux.
- **Ipamorelin:** Never FDA-approved. Reviewed by FDA PCAC in **October 2024** for GHD and postoperative ileus. FDA noted significant safety concerns about the use of certain peptides in compounded products and elected to evaluate ipamorelin on its own initiative due to these concerns.
- Both peptides are currently available via **503A compounding pharmacies** in the US with a physician's prescription, but regulatory access may change as PCAC decisions are finalised.
- **FDA FAERS review:** No serious adverse events directly attributable to CJC-1295 acetate compounded products have been identified through June 2024. Ipamorelin FAERS data similarly limited.
- **Outside the US:** Regulatory status varies by jurisdiction; used in wellness and anti-aging clinics in many markets.

> ⚠️ **Key regulatory warning:** The peptide compounding landscape in the US is under active regulatory review. Users and prescribers should monitor FDA guidance closely. Access to compounded CJC-1295 and ipamorelin may be restricted or require different sourcing in the future.

## Education Hook Facts

> These are punchy, quiz-interstitial-ready facts:

1. **Your body has two "gas pedals" for releasing growth hormone — this stack presses both at once.** CJC-1295 activates the GHRH receptor; ipamorelin activates the ghrelin receptor. The result is a GH pulse that's larger than either could trigger alone.

2. **Ipamorelin was the first GHRP that didn't make you hungry or spike cortisol.** Earlier growth hormone peptides (GHRP-6) caused significant appetite surges. Ipamorelin was specifically engineered to be selective — only the growth hormone signal, none of the noise.

3. **After age 30, GH levels drop by about 15% per decade.** By 50, many adults are operating with a fraction of their youthful GH output. The CJC-1295 + ipamorelin stack targets this decline by stimulating the pituitary gland rather than replacing GH from outside.

4. **IGF-1 is the primary measurable marker.** You can't directly measure GH pulses at home, but IGF-1 — elevated for 12–24 hours after each GH pulse — shows up on a standard blood panel. Clinicians use IGF-1 to confirm the stack is working and to calibrate dosing.

5. **This combination is almost always dosed at night — on purpose.** The majority of daily GH secretion happens during deep sleep. Dosing before bed means the CJC-1295 + ipamorelin pulse coincides with the body's natural GH window, amplifying the nocturnal surge rather than creating an artificial one.

## Dosing Reference

> ⚠️ For research and educational reference only. Requires licensed physician oversight and baseline IGF-1 blood work before starting.

**Standard combined protocol (most common clinical approach):**

| Peptide | Dose | Frequency | Route |
|---|---|---|---|
| CJC-1295 (no DAC) | 100–300 mcg | Once nightly | Subcutaneous injection |
| Ipamorelin | 100–300 mcg | Once nightly (same injection) | Subcutaneous injection |

**Common pre-mixed formulation:** Many compounding pharmacies supply CJC-1295 + ipamorelin in a single combined vial (e.g., 2000 mcg/mL CJC-1295 + 2000 mcg/mL ipamorelin). A typical starting dose is 0.1 mL (200 mcg of each), titrated upward based on IGF-1 response and tolerance.

**Timing:** 60–90 minutes after last meal, at bedtime. Avoid eating after injection — insulin (raised by food) suppresses GH release.

**Escalation protocol (example from Perfect B / clinical observation):**
- Week 1: 0.6 mg combined (lower end; acclimatisation)
- Weeks 2–4: Titrate up to 0.8–1.0 mg based on tolerance and IGF-1 labs

**Cycle length:** Typically 3–6 months on, 1–2 months off. Some protocols run 5 days on / 2 days off (weekday dosing) to mimic natural GH variation and avoid pituitary desensitisation.

**CJC-1295 with DAC (less common protocol):**
- Dose: 1000–2000 mcg once or twice weekly (subcutaneous)
- Not recommended for most clinical anti-aging protocols due to non-pulsatile GH profile and greater regulatory uncertainty

**Monitoring:**
- **Baseline IGF-1** before starting
- **Follow-up IGF-1** at 6–8 weeks; target upper-third of age-appropriate reference range
- Fasting glucose monitoring (especially in pre-diabetics)
- Dose adjustment based on labs and symptom response
