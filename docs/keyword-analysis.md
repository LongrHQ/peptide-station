## Summary & Key Insights

### 1. BPC-157 Is the Undisputed Category King
At **301,000 monthly US searches** with a $6.39 CPC, BPC-157 is by far the highest-demand peptide in the catalogue. This single keyword dwarfs most competitors. Its related keyword "bpc 157 peptide" adds another 110,000/month at LOW competition (18/100) — meaning the branded search term is highly contested but the descriptive variant is an SEO opportunity. **BPC-157 should be the #1 quiz recommendation weight.**

### 2. GLP-1 Drugs Are Dominating with Sky-High CPCs
**Tirzepatide** has 1,000,000+ monthly US searches with a $23.66 CPC — the single highest-volume keyword in the entire set. **Compounded semaglutide** (49,500 searches, $27.05 CPC) and **semaglutide injection** (22,200 searches, $31.23 CPC — highest CPC in the dataset) confirm that the GLP-1 space is a gold rush. "Peptide for weight loss" is surging too at 74,000/month (+271% over 12 months). These are GLP-1 agonists but the market is enormous and reflects huge commercial intent.

### 3. Sermorelin Is the Highest-CPC "Classic" Peptide
**Sermorelin** has 165,000 monthly searches and a **$14.02 CPC** — far above all other non-GLP-1 peptides. This suggests significant paid competition from compounding pharmacies and telehealth providers. **Sermorelin vs CJC-1295** also has a $9.96 CPC on just 1,000 searches, indicating extremely high buyer intent on comparison queries.

### 4. GHK-Cu is Massively Undervalued by Competitors
**GHK-Cu** (copper peptide) has **110,000 monthly searches** but only a **$3.80 CPC** — roughly half the CPC of BPC-157 and less than a third of sermorelin. This is one of the best value opportunities in the catalogue: enormous demand, relatively cheap to advertise against.

### 5. AOD-9604 / IGF-1 LR3 Are Performance Stealth Stars
**AOD-9604** (14,800 searches, $7.48 CPC) and **AOD-9604 peptide** (18,100 searches, $8.51 CPC — counterintuitively higher volume than the base term) are strong performers in fat loss/performance. **IGF-1 LR3** is also surging hard: 12,100 average but **27,100 in March 2026** (+400% vs. 12 months prior). High commercial intent (HIGH competition at CPC $5.21).

### 6. PT-141 Is Surprising — 33,100 Searches at Low Cost
**PT-141** (bremelanotide/libido peptide) has **33,100 monthly US searches** with a $6.55 CPC at maximum competition (100/100). Despite the sensitive category, this is a very large market. PT-141 peptide adds another 18,100/month. Generic terms like "peptide for libido" return zero volume — the market searches for the specific product, not the category.

### 7. Longevity-Specific Peptides Are Niche But Growing
Epitalon, epitalon peptide, and epitalon anti-aging all returned **zero volume** — likely too niche for Google Ads data to register. However, **mots-c peptide** (27,100 searches, $8.11 CPC at LOW competition) and **ss-31 peptide** (14,800 searches, $5.76 CPC at LOW competition) represent genuine underserved opportunities. High search volume + low competition = SEO/content goldmine.

### 8. "Peptide for Muscle Growth" Exploded — 90K in March 2026
This generic intent keyword hit **90,500 searches in March 2026** (vs. 14,800 in April 2025 = 511% increase). Monthly average is 40,500. CPC $3.96 at HIGH competition. This reflects the mainstream peptide moment happening right now.

### 9. Peptide for Hair Growth Is Surging
**12,100 searches/month** average, up from 5,400 twelve months ago (+124%). CPC $2.96 at HIGH competition. Hair loss is a top-of-funnel concern that peptides (GHK-Cu, sermorelin) can address.

### 10. Most Long-Tail "Peptide For X" Terms Have Low Volume But High CPC
"Peptide for gut healing", "peptide for skin rejuvenation", "peptide for injury recovery", "peptide for fat burning" all returned **zero Google Ads volume** — meaning either too niche for their index or people use different search patterns. However, terms like "peptide for energy" ($8.17 CPC!) and "peptide for inflammation" ($6.41 CPC) show disproportionately high CPC relative to their modest volume (2,400/month each), signalling buyer intent.
