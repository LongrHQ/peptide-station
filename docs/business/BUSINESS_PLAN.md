# Peptide Station — Business Plan

> Version 1.0 | March 2026 | Confidential

---

## Executive Summary

Peptide Station is an e-commerce and educational platform selling therapeutic peptide products exclusively in **pen delivery format** — pre-mixed, auto-injector cartridges that remove the friction of traditional peptide injection.

The market is large ($46B+ in clinical peptides alone), growing fast (8–10% CAGR), and the consumer end is dominated by grey-market vendors selling confusing, poorly presented, inadequately tested research chemicals. There is a clear gap for a **trusted, design-forward, education-first** brand.

Peptide Station will position as the Apple of peptide pens: beautiful UX, premium but accessible, backed by rigorous quality assurance and a content platform that educates as much as it sells.

**Key metrics targeted by end of Year 3:**
- $3.5M+ annual revenue
- 15,000+ active customers
- 50,000+ email subscribers
- 500+ monthly content platform users

---

## Problem Statement

### The Peptide Consumer's Current Reality

1. **Grey-market dominance.** The vast majority of peptide vendors operate as "research chemical" sellers — technically legal, but with zero accountability, inconsistent quality, and products designed for labs, not people. COA fraud is rampant (outliyr.com found many common research-grade products have fake COAs, low purity, or high contaminants).

2. **Delivery is archaic.** Most peptides are sold as lyophilised powder in vials, requiring the user to source bacteriostatic water, reconstitute correctly, draw into a syringe, and inject — a multi-step process that is confusing, error-prone, and unnecessarily clinical. The auto-injector pen format (think Ozempic) exists but very few vendors use it.

3. **Zero education.** The typical peptide vendor website has a product page, a COA link, and a disclaimer. There is almost no quality educational content explaining mechanisms, protocols, stacking, safety, or expected outcomes.

4. **Trust deficit.** Biohackers and longevity enthusiasts are increasingly curious about peptides (NYT, Guardian, CNN coverage in 2025/2026) but find the current market intimidating and opaque.

5. **Regulatory confusion.** The legal landscape (research chemical vs. medicinal product, FDA enforcement, DEA scheduling) is confusing for consumers and vendors alike. Most vendors paper over this with a "for research use only" disclaimer and move on.

### The Opportunity Gap

**Demand is surging:**
- BPC 157 searches: 301,000/month in the US alone
- GHK-Cu: 110,000/month (explosive 5x growth from March 2025)
- Sermorelin: 165,000/month (3x growth over 12 months)
- "Peptide pens" as a term: 10x growth in 12 months (480 → 4,400/month)

**Supply is inadequate:** No brand currently owns the "trusted peptide pen" category. Limitless Biotech and Peptide Sciences (US) are closest to a quality-focused model, but neither is pen-first — their branding is still research-chemical-adjacent, educational content is thin, and they don't provide the consumer-grade experience Peptide Station will deliver.

## Solution

Peptide Station delivers a **vertically integrated pen peptide experience:**

1. **Product:** Pre-mixed peptide pens in the most in-demand compounds, delivered with everything needed (pen device, needles, swabs, sharps bin, guide)
2. **Education:** A content platform that teaches — guides per peptide, dosing calculators, stacking guides, safety protocols, video walkthroughs
3. **Trust:** Published COAs, named US/EU sourcing, no fake research framing — honest about what these are and who uses them
4. **Subscription:** Pen refill subscriptions for ongoing protocols (CJC-1295/Ipamorelin cycles, BPC-157 healing protocols, etc.)
5. **Community:** Forum/content community for protocol sharing, Q&A, progress tracking

## Revenue Model

### 1. Direct Product Sales (Core)

Pre-mixed peptide pen kits and cartridge refills. Margin structure:
- Raw peptide cost (from US/EU manufacturer): $3–20 per 5mg vial depending on compound
- Pen device + kit assembly: $7–15 per unit
- Target selling price: $40–150 per unit
- **Gross margin: 50–70%**

### 2. Subscription — Pen Refill Protocols

Monthly or quarterly subscription for protocol refills. Example:
- "The Builder Stack" (CJC-1295 + Ipamorelin): $119/month subscription
- "Recovery Protocol" (BPC-157 + TB-500): $99/month
- "Longevity Stack" (Sermorelin + GHK-Cu): $129/month

Subscription reduces churn, improves LTV. Target: 30% of revenue from subscriptions by Year 2.

### 3. Starter Kits (Acquisition Product)

First-time pen kit including device, needles, swabs, sharps bin, guide, and first cartridge. Priced to be compelling entry point, then upgrade to subscription.

### 4. Educational Content (Phase 2+)

- Free: Blog, YouTube, podcast content (SEO and trust-building)
- Paid: "Peptide Academy" — course on peptide protocols, safety, science ($97–197 one-time)
- Professional: Practitioner certification course ($497+)

> Note: All educational pricing in USD; US is the primary content market.

### 5. Affiliate Revenue

Partner with longevity clinics, health coaches, biohacking influencers. 15–20% commission structure. Drives low-cost acquisition.

## Competitive Advantages

1. **Pen-first product design** — The only US brand built exclusively around the pen format. Others treat pens as a secondary SKU or don't offer them at all.

2. **Education as moat** — If we own the "BPC-157 explained" content space, we own discovery and trust. The educational moat compounds over time (SEO authority, email list, YouTube subscribers).

3. **Brand design** — "Peptide Station" is warm, approachable, human. The category is cold and clinical. This is a positioning gap.

4. **Longr ecosystem** — Cross-referral from Longevity Direct patients, Saints One supplement buyers. Shared audience without direct competition.

5. **US-sourced, US-tested peptides** — US-manufactured or US third-party tested peptides build consumer trust vs. foreign imports. USP-grade materials and FDA cGMP-compliant manufacturing referenced in all marketing.

6. **Subscription model** — Creates recurring revenue and reduces churn. Most competitors are pure transactional.

## Key Risks & Mitigations

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|------------|--------|------------|
| FDA enforcement on peptide sales (research use framing) | High | High | Maintain strict "research use only" framing; engage US regulatory counsel (Frier Levitt); monitor FDA warning letters |
| FDA escalates to seizures/injunctions | Medium | Very High | Ensure marketing never implies human use; no health claims; separate US legal entity |
| Competitor copies pen format | High | Medium | Build brand and content moat first; first-mover advantage in US pen segment |
| Quality/COA fraud in supply chain | Low | Very High | Use named US/EU manufacturers; own-batch third-party testing (USP-grade) |
| Peptide trend fades | Low | High | Diversify into proven, research-backed compounds; education helps long-term |
| Payment processor issues | High | High | Use peptide-friendly US processors (Corepay, AllayPay, Paynote); crypto backup; diversify |
| State-by-state regulatory variation | Medium | Medium | Monitor state pharmacy boards; avoid direct-to-consumer claims in high-enforcement states |
| WADA/sports doping association | Low | Medium | Disclaim suitability for competitive athletes; BPC-157 is WADA-prohibited |
