# Peptide Station — Product Strategy

---

## Core Product Philosophy

**Pen first, peptide second.**

Most peptide vendors are peptide-first: "Here is our BPC-157 catalogue." Peptide Station is pen-first: "Here is the easiest, most trusted way to administer BPC-157."

Every product decision flows from this: the pen is the UX, the peptide is the ingredient. We're building something closer to a pharmaceutical delivery system than a research chemical shop.

## Peptide Selection Framework

### Selection Criteria

1. **Demand:** High search volume / consumer interest (backed by keyword data)
2. **Legal clarity:** Can be sold as research chemicals in the US without prescription (not on FDA's compounding prohibited list for research use)
3. **Pen-suitability:** Stable enough in pre-mixed form; subcutaneous delivery appropriate
4. **Margin:** Sufficient margin at accessible price points
5. **Evidence base:** Meaningful research body to support educational content

## Pen Device Options

### The Market

Pen delivery systems fall into two categories:

**1. Reusable Insulin-Type Pens (Cartridge-Based)**
- Standard format: 3mL cartridge, adjustable dose dial
- Used by: Express Peptides (UK), Direct Peptides (international), Limitless Biotech (US)
- Device examples: PepPen V2 (solid metal, 0.01mL increments, 28–33G needles), similar insulin pen format
- Mechanism: User dials dose, cap removed from needle, inject subcutaneously, cap replaced
- Refills: Separate cartridge replacements

**2. Pre-Filled Disposable Pens**
- Pre-loaded, single-use or fixed-use (e.g., Ozempic-style)
- Higher per-unit cost but simpler UX
- Better for premium/gifting, worse for recurring buyers
- Ypsomed FixPen and VarioJect are clinical-grade options

### Peptide Station Recommendation

**Launch with reusable pen + cartridge model:**
- Starter Kit: Pen device + 5 needle tips + alcohol swabs + sharps bin + first cartridge
- Refill: Cartridge only (cheaper, drives repeat purchase)
- Device: White-label a quality metal pen similar to PepPen V2 format — solid, premium feel
- Custom branding: Peptide Station engraved/branded pen as hero product

**Device sourcing options:**
- PepPen (thepeppen.com) — US-based, already sells to research community; good starting point
- Generic insulin pen ODM manufacturers (Alibaba/Chinese OEM, but quality check required)
- Custom OEM via US medical device contract manufacturer (e.g., West Pharmaceutical Services, Phillips Medisize US) — higher cost, better FDA optics
- Ypsomed — clinical-grade (likely over-engineered for launch, but aspirational)

**Pen specs:**
- Metal body (premium feel vs. plastic)
- 60-unit or 80-unit dial (0.5mL to 0.6mL capacity)
- Compatible with standard 3mL cartridges
- Universal 28–33G pen needles
- Travel case included in starter kit

## Sourcing & Manufacturing

### Peptide Sourcing Tiers

**Tier 1 (Preferred): US-Manufactured or US Third-Party Tested**
- US peptide synthesis labs (e.g., American Peptide Company, Bachem Americas, GenScript USA)
- Advantages: No import issues, USP/FDA cGMP-aligned documentation, trust signal for US consumers, stronger brand narrative
- Third-party testing at US-accredited labs (e.g., Eurofins USA, Analytical Biologics, Alcami)
- Disadvantages: Higher cost per mg

**Tier 2: EU-Manufactured**
- European peptide synthesis labs (Germany, Netherlands have strong pharma-grade facilities)
- Good price/quality balance; reputable origin for US consumers
- Import documentation required; FDA can flag foreign-manufactured research chemicals at customs

**Tier 3: Chinese API Manufacture (not recommended for launch)**
- Chinese peptide manufacturers dominate global supply (Genscript, LifeTein, etc.)
- Quality can be excellent but:
  - Consumer trust lower in US market post-NYT "Chinese Peptides" article (Jan 2026)
  - Import documentation complexity; FDA scrutiny of Chinese-origin research chemicals increasing
  - Not aligned with premium brand positioning

### Quality Assurance

**Non-negotiable standards:**
- HPLC purity ≥96% minimum; ≥99% preferred (USP-grade reference)
- Third-party COA from US-accredited lab for every batch (Eurofins USA, Analytical Biologics, or equivalent)
- Published COAs on product pages (builds trust, differentiates from grey-market)
- Endotoxin testing (LPS) — contamination major safety concern for injectables
- Heavy metal screening
- Microbial testing
- Manufacturing at FDA cGMP-compliant facilities where possible

**Inspiration:** Limitless Biotech is ranked #1 by outliyr.com specifically because of their rigorous third-party testing. Peptide Station aims to match or exceed this standard as a US-first brand.

**Reference standard:** "HPLC purity ≥96% is the minimum for research-grade; ≥99% is excellent" (outliyr.com research)

### Pre-Mixed Pen Production

Pre-mixing peptide with bacteriostatic water before filling cartridges:
- **Stability:** Pre-mixed peptides stable for 30 days at 4°C (refrigerated)
- **Shelf life:** Lyophilised powder 12–24 months; pre-mixed 30–60 days if refrigerated
- **Cold chain:** Pre-mixed pens MUST be cold-chain shipped (2–8°C transit)
- **Cost implication:** Requires refrigerated packaging (gel packs, insulated box)
- **Logistics:** 2-day delivery with cold pack via FedEx/UPS — feasible across CONUS; Alaska/Hawaii may need dry ice or lyophilised-only option

**Alternative:** Sell lyophilised powder in vials + separate bacteriostatic water + pen device as a kit. User mixes themselves. Simpler logistics (no cold chain for powder), but adds user complexity. Hybrid approach recommended initially.

### Regulatory Note on Manufacturing

Peptides sold as research chemicals don't require pharmaceutical manufacturing standards (GMP), but voluntarily meeting/aspiring to higher standards is a trust differentiator and may be required if ever pivoting to clinical/prescription status.
