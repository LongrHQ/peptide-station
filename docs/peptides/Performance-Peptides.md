# Performance Peptides

> **Category file:** IGF-1 LR3 · Follistatin 344 · AOD-9604
>
> These peptides target body composition, muscle development, and fat metabolism. They are among the most discussed performance and physique peptides in both clinical research and the biohacker/athlete community. Evidence quality ranges from mixed human clinical trials (AOD-9604) to animal/mechanistic predominant (Follistatin 344) to research-only with WADA prohibition (IGF-1 LR3).

---

## IGF-1 LR3

**Category:** Performance / Anabolic / Growth factor
**Also known as:** Long R3 IGF-1, Insulin-like Growth Factor-1 Long Arg3, LR3-IGF-1, R3-IGF-1

### Overview & Mechanism

IGF-1 LR3 (Insulin-like Growth Factor-1 Long Arg3) is a synthetic, recombinant analogue of endogenous IGF-1, engineered with two key structural modifications: (1) an Arginine substitution at position 3 (replacing glutamic acid), and (2) a 13-amino-acid N-terminal extension. These changes dramatically reduce binding to IGF-binding proteins (IGFBPs), which normally sequester ~98% of circulating IGF-1 in the bloodstream, limiting its receptor activity. The result is a peptide with a **half-life of approximately 20–30 hours** (vs 12–15 hours for native IGF-1), significantly greater receptor bioavailability, and up to **2-fold higher anabolic potency** in animal infusion studies compared to native IGF-1.

The primary mechanism involves binding to the IGF-1 receptor (IGF1R) on skeletal muscle, connective tissue, and other tissues, activating the **PI3K/Akt/mTOR signalling cascade** — the central pathway governing muscle protein synthesis, satellite cell activation, and hypertrophy. Downstream effects include: increased muscle fibre cross-sectional area, activation of satellite cells (muscle stem cells) to support fibre repair and new muscle formation, collagen synthesis and tendon repair, improved nutrient partitioning (glucose uptake into muscle cells), and anti-catabolic effects during caloric restriction. Unlike growth hormone, IGF-1 LR3 acts downstream and does not require hepatic conversion, meaning its effects are more direct and tissue-specific.

### Primary Use Cases

- **Muscle hypertrophy:** Primary use in bodybuilding/physique community for lean mass gains
- **Accelerated recovery:** Faster repair of damaged muscle and connective tissue post-training
- **Improved nutrient partitioning:** Enhanced glucose and amino acid uptake by muscle cells
- **Injury rehabilitation:** Collagen synthesis and tendon/ligament healing (research context)
- **Sarcopenia research:** Potential therapeutic use in age-related muscle loss (preclinical)
- **Bone density:** Animal studies show IGF-1 signalling improvements in bone mineral density (Yakar & Rosen, JBMR)

### Evidence Quality

**Moderate animal evidence; limited human research; no FDA-approved anabolic indication.**

IGF-1 signalling and its role in muscle growth is exceptionally well-characterised in basic science. Animal studies (continuous infusion models) demonstrate 2-fold anabolic superiority over native IGF-1. Human research is limited by ethical constraints and WADA prohibition — the compound cannot be tested in elite athletes. Available human data comes from clinical use of native IGF-1 in growth hormone deficiency and certain wasting conditions, with pharmacological extrapolation to LR3. The analytical detection methodology has been published (Thomas et al., 2017, Journal of Pharmaceutical and Biomedical Analysis). Evidence strength: **Strong mechanistic basis; human anabolic efficacy data largely extrapolated from animal studies and IGF-1 pathway literature.**

### Quiz Signals

- ✅ Primary goal is **maximum muscle hypertrophy** — building lean mass beyond what standard training and nutrition deliver
- ✅ Experiencing **training plateau** despite consistent programming and diet — looking for a pharmacological catalyst
- ✅ Interested in **tissue repair and injury recovery** — particularly connective tissue, tendon, or ligament damage
- ✅ Understands and accepts the **anabolic risk profile** — not a beginner looking for a gentle entry-level peptide
- ✅ Has prior experience with other peptides (GHRP/CJC, BPC-157) and seeking to **advance their protocol**
- ✅ Goal includes **nutrient partitioning and body recomposition** — shifting calories toward muscle rather than fat

### Risks & Regulatory Notes

- **WADA Prohibited — S2 (Peptide Hormones, Growth Factors, Related Substances):** IGF-1 in all exogenous forms is prohibited at all times, both in and out of competition. Athletes face career-ending consequences if detected.
- **Cancer risk:** IGF-1R is expressed on most cancer cell types, and elevated IGF-1 signalling is associated with increased proliferation of tumour cells. This is the most serious long-term concern.
- **Hypoglycaemia:** IGF-1 LR3 has insulin-like effects on glucose uptake; hypoglycaemia is a real risk, especially without proper dosing and carbohydrate management
- **Organomegaly:** Chronic high-dose IGF-1 can stimulate growth of internal organs (heart, intestines, kidneys) — a serious irreversible risk associated with GH/IGF-1 abuse
- **Acromegaly-like symptoms:** Jaw and facial bone growth, joint pain at high doses
- **Nerve compression symptoms:** Carpal tunnel syndrome and related neuropathies
- **Oedema:** Fluid retention, especially in extremities
- **Not FDA approved for performance use:** No approved human indication for anabolic/body composition use
- **Short detection window:** Anti-doping test detection is possible via LC-MS analysis (Thomas et al., 2017) but window varies

### Education Hook Facts

1. **IGF-1 LR3 was specifically engineered to escape the body's natural "off-switch" for IGF-1** — the binding proteins that normally limit its activity. The result is a molecule up to 2× more anabolic than the natural hormone in animal studies.
2. **WADA banned IGF-1 in all forms after the Vijay Singh case** — the golf world's highest-paid player was investigated in 2014 for using deer antler spray (which contains IGF-1), triggering WADA to explicitly clarify its prohibited status.
3. **Skeletal muscle contains IGF-1 receptors on virtually every fibre** — which is why IGF-1 signalling is the central molecular mechanism behind hypertrophy from both training and anabolic compounds.

### Dosing Reference

**Research/biohacker community protocol (no FDA-approved protocol):**
- **Dose:** 20–100 mcg per day subcutaneous injection
- **Timing:** Post-workout or split into AM/PM doses
- **Cycle duration:** 4–6 weeks maximum (receptor downregulation occurs with prolonged use)
- **Rest period:** At least 4–6 weeks off between cycles

*Note: Dosing above 100 mcg/day significantly increases hypoglycaemia and organ growth risk. This compound requires careful glucose monitoring. Given WADA prohibition and cancer risk profile, medical supervision is strongly advised.*

## Follistatin 344

**Category:** Performance / Muscle development / TGF-β regulation
**Also known as:** FST-344, Follistatin isoform 344, FS-344

### Overview & Mechanism

Follistatin 344 is a specific isoform of the naturally occurring glycoprotein Follistatin (encoded by the FST gene), characterised by 344 amino acids. Follistatin functions as a **TGF-β superfamily antagonist**, binding to and neutralising multiple TGF-β ligands — most importantly **myostatin (GDF-8)**, the body's primary brake on skeletal muscle growth. By blocking myostatin, follistatin removes a significant ceiling on muscle hypertrophy and regeneration capacity. It also antagonises activin A (implicated in muscle wasting and inflammation) and several BMPs, broadening its effects on tissue remodelling.

The "double muscling" phenomenon in cattle (Belgian Blue breed) — producing extraordinarily muscled animals — results from natural myostatin loss-of-function mutations, validating the myostatin pathway as a major determinant of muscle mass (McPherron & Lee, PNAS, 1997). In mice, AAV1-delivered follistatin-344 gene therapy produced significant increases in muscle size and strength in both healthy and mdx (muscular dystrophy) models (Rodino-Klapac et al., Molecular Therapy, 2013). Beyond myostatin inhibition, follistatin activates the **Akt/mTOR pathway** independently, creating a dual anabolic effect through both disinhibition and direct stimulation of protein synthesis. It also promotes satellite cell (muscle stem cell) proliferation, supporting regenerative capacity (Gilson et al., Journal of Cell Science, 2009).

### Primary Use Cases

- **Extreme muscle hypertrophy:** The most powerful known myostatin inhibitor — theoretically removes the genetic ceiling on muscle mass
- **Muscular dystrophy research:** Gene therapy context (AAV-delivered FS-344) for Duchenne and Becker MD
- **Age-related muscle loss (sarcopenia):** Restoring muscle mass in older adults by relieving myostatin-mediated suppression
- **Athletic performance enhancement:** Physique-focused and strength athletes seeking muscle mass beyond natural limits
- **Fertility/ovarian function:** Follistatin plays a role in follicular development; investigated in reproductive medicine (off-target to muscle use)
- **Body recomposition:** Improving lean mass to fat ratio

### Evidence Quality

**Strong animal evidence; gene therapy human data exists for MD; peptide injection data very limited.**

The myostatin/follistatin biology is exceptionally well-established in basic science and animal models. Gene therapy delivery (not injectable peptide) has human Phase I/II data in Becker muscular dystrophy. Recombinant follistatin protein research in animals shows robust muscle hypertrophy. **Critical caveat:** Most of the biohacker market uses injectable recombinant follistatin-344 protein — this is a very different delivery mechanism than gene therapy or native follistatin. The bioavailability, distribution, and half-life of injected recombinant FS-344 protein in humans is poorly characterised. No published human clinical trials of injectable recombinant follistatin-344 for performance exist. Evidence strength: **Strong pathway biology; clinical evidence for injection-based use is minimal — significant gap between animal data and human application.**

### Quiz Signals

- ✅ Primary goal is **maximum, near-genetic-ceiling muscle growth** — normal peptides and training aren't enough
- ✅ Has a diagnosed or suspected **muscular dystrophy** or muscle wasting condition — seeking relevant research pathways
- ✅ Dealing with **severe sarcopenia** or age-related muscle loss unresponsive to conventional interventions
- ✅ Very advanced user who has **exhausted other anabolic peptide options** (IGF-1 LR3, growth hormone secretagogues) and is researching the next layer
- ✅ Interested in the **myostatin biology** specifically — understands the double-muscling cattle model and wants that pathway targeted
- ✅ Willing to accept **very limited human safety data** in pursuit of extraordinary muscle hypertrophy outcomes

### Risks & Regulatory Notes

- **Reproductive effects:** Follistatin regulates activin, which controls FSH secretion — injected follistatin could disrupt the hypothalamic-pituitary-gonadal axis and affect fertility (particularly in women)
- **Ovarian hyperstimulation:** A real risk in females — follistatin is involved in follicular development
- **Cancer risk:** Follistatin promotes cell proliferation broadly; elevated follistatin expression is associated with several cancers (ovarian, prostate, others)
- **Unknown systemic effects of injected recombinant protein:** Unlike a small peptide, follistatin-344 is a ~40 kDa glycoprotein — its distribution, degradation products, and off-target binding in the human body are not well-characterised
- **Immunogenicity:** Recombinant proteins are more likely than short peptides to trigger antibody responses
- **Not FDA approved for performance use**
- **WADA:** Follistatin/myostatin modulators fall under S2 growth factors and the general prohibition on growth factor pathway modulators. Status: likely prohibited as a "growth factor" or "related substance" — athletes should seek specific WADA clarification.
- **Organ size concerns:** As a broad TGF-β antagonist, off-target tissue growth is theoretically possible

### Education Hook Facts

1. **Belgian Blue cattle have naturally occurring myostatin mutations** — they produce such extreme muscle mass that calves are often born by Caesarean section because their muscles are too large for natural birth. This is the live demonstration of what follistatin inhibits.
2. **Follistatin doesn't just block one muscle-growth brake — it blocks several** TGF-β family members simultaneously, which is why its anabolic effect in animal models is additive to myostatin knockout alone.
3. **A gene therapy approach using AAV-delivered follistatin-344 has been tested in human patients with Becker muscular dystrophy**, making it one of the few performance-adjacent peptides to have crossed into human clinical investigation.

### Dosing Reference

**Research community protocol for injectable recombinant FS-344:**
- **Dose:** 100–300 mcg per day subcutaneous injection
- **Cycle duration:** 10–30 days (short cycles due to concerns about sustained receptor effects)
- **Frequency:** Daily during the cycle

*Critical note: This is an entirely community-derived protocol with no clinical validation. The pharmacokinetics of injected recombinant follistatin-344 in humans are unknown. The female reproductive risk profile warrants extreme caution. This is one of the higher-risk peptides in terms of off-target systemic effects.*

## AOD-9604

**Category:** Performance / Fat loss / Growth hormone fragment
**Also known as:** Anti-Obesity Drug 9604, HGH Fragment 176-191, AOD9604

### Overview & Mechanism

AOD-9604 is a synthetic peptide fragment derived from the **C-terminal region (amino acids 176–191) of human growth hormone**, with an added tyrosine residue at the N-terminus to improve stability. It was originally developed by Metabolic Pharmaceuticals in Australia specifically to isolate the fat-metabolising properties of growth hormone without the glucose-elevating, IGF-1-stimulating, and anabolic effects of full-length GH. Critically, **AOD-9604 does not bind to the growth hormone receptor**, which is why it lacks most of GH's systemic effects.

Its mechanism of fat loss is dual: (1) it **stimulates lipolysis** — the breakdown of stored fat — by upregulating beta-3 adrenergic receptors in adipose tissue (which are typically suppressed in obesity), and (2) it **inhibits lipogenesis** — the conversion of excess calories into new fat stores. Unlike GLP-1 drugs, AOD-9604 does not affect appetite; its action is purely at the level of adipose tissue metabolism. It has no effect on serum IGF-1, carbohydrate metabolism, or insulin sensitivity — a key safety advantage confirmed across all six human clinical trials. Animal studies also suggest potential effects on cartilage repair and regeneration (via GH receptor-independent pathways), which has sparked interest in its use for joint health.

### Primary Use Cases

- **Targeted fat loss:** Primary use, especially for stubborn visceral and subcutaneous fat
- **Body composition:** Improving lean-to-fat ratio without the GH side effect profile
- **Metabolic health:** Adjunct to weight management protocols where glucose or insulin concerns limit GH use
- **Joint health / cartilage:** Emerging interest based on in vitro and animal cartilage regeneration data
- **Post-GH protocol "step-down":** Used by biohackers transitioning from full GH cycles to maintain some fat-mobilisation benefit
- **Combination with GLP-1 drugs:** Some practitioners use alongside semaglutide or tirzepatide for additive fat loss with complementary mechanisms

### Evidence Quality

**Mixed: strong safety data from six human trials; efficacy data disappointing at primary endpoint.**

AOD-9604 completed six human clinical trials between 2001 and 2006 involving approximately 925 participants — an unusually complete clinical dataset for a research peptide. Safety findings were excellent: no serious adverse events, no antibody formation, no IGF-1 effects. However, the pivotal Phase IIb trial (METAOD005, 12-week RCT, n=~500) showed ~2.6 kg weight loss vs ~0.8 kg placebo — a trend but **not statistically significant at the primary endpoint**, leading the program to be discontinued as a pharmaceutical drug in 2007 (Wittert et al., Diabetes, 2005). The compound subsequently received **GRAS (Generally Recognized as Safe) status from the FDA as a food ingredient** — a separate regulatory category from pharmaceutical approval. Evidence strength: **Excellent safety profile confirmed in humans; primary fat-loss efficacy not statistically proven in pivotal trial.**

### Quiz Signals

- ✅ Goal is **targeted fat loss**, particularly visceral fat or stubborn deposits that haven't responded to diet/exercise
- ✅ Wants the **fat-metabolising benefits of growth hormone** without GH's effects on glucose, IGF-1, or organ growth
- ✅ Has **insulin sensitivity concerns or pre-diabetes** that make full GH protocols inadvisable
- ✅ Seeking a peptide with **the most complete human safety dataset** among research fat-loss compounds
- ✅ Currently using or considering **GLP-1 medications** and wants to understand complementary fat-metabolism mechanisms
- ✅ Interested in potential **joint/cartilage health benefits** alongside fat loss

### Risks & Regulatory Notes

- **Pivotal human trial missed primary endpoint:** This is important — the Phase IIb trial did not show statistically significant weight loss. Individual responses may vary, but population-level efficacy was not proven
- **FDA Category 2 (compounding):** Alongside Epitalon, MOTS-c, and others — FDA has raised safety concerns about compounded AOD-9604 injectables
- **Not FDA approved as a drug:** The GRAS designation is for food ingredient use, not injectable therapy
- **No effect on lean mass:** Unlike GH, does not build muscle — purely a lipolytic agent
- **Injection site reactions:** Mild and transient; most common adverse event
- **WADA:** Not explicitly listed on the Prohibited List, but could fall under growth factor-related substance interpretation; athletes should seek specific guidance
- **Regulatory grey zone:** Legal to possess as a research compound in most jurisdictions; not approved for human therapeutic use in the US, UK, or EU
- **Contraindications:** Active cancer (due to fat mobilisation effects), pregnancy, breastfeeding

### Education Hook Facts

1. **AOD-9604 is the only fragment of human growth hormone to have undergone six full human clinical trials** — making its safety profile among the most human-validated of any research peptide.
2. **Unlike GH itself, AOD-9604 has zero effect on blood glucose or insulin** — making it uniquely safe for metabolically compromised individuals who cannot use full growth hormone protocols.
3. **The pivotal Phase IIb trial showed 2.6 kg fat loss vs 0.8 kg placebo in 12 weeks** — a real but statistically inconclusive signal, which ultimately killed its pharmaceutical development but kept clinical interest alive.

### Dosing Reference

**Clinical trial and community protocol:**
- **Dose:** 250–500 mcg per day subcutaneous injection (clinical trial range: 1 mg/day in some trials)
- **Timing:** Morning, fasted, or 30 minutes before exercise for maximum lipolytic effect
- **Cycle duration:** 12–24 weeks (consistent with clinical trial durations)
- **Route:** Subcutaneous injection; intranasal and oral forms exist but with significantly lower bioavailability

*Note: The clinical trial pivotal dose was 1 mg/day oral. The injectable community dose of 250–500 mcg/day is extrapolated from this, accounting for improved bioavailability via subcutaneous route. Some practitioners use 250 mcg twice daily.*
