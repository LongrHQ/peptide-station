# TB-500 (Thymosin Beta-4 Fragment)

**Category:** Tissue Repair, Mobility & Systemic Recovery
**Also known as:** TB500, Thymosin Beta-4 Fragment, Tβ4 fragment, Ac-LKKTETQ (active fragment sequence), TB-4 (used interchangeably, though technically Tβ4 = full protein)
**Source:** RBT Supplier Catalogue + Tavily Research
**Last updated:** 2026-04-09

---

## Overview

TB-500 is a synthetic peptide representing the active fragment of Thymosin Beta-4 (Tβ4) — specifically the actin-binding domain with the amino acid sequence **Ac-Leu-Lys-Lys-Thr-Glu-Thr-Gln (Ac-LKKTETQ)**. This heptapeptide (7 amino acids) is derived from the full 43-amino acid Tβ4 protein and retains many of its regenerative properties in a more compact, synthetically accessible form.

**The parent molecule — Thymosin Beta-4 (Tβ4):** Naturally occurring in virtually all human and animal cells (with the exception of red blood cells), Tβ4 is the most abundant member of the β-thymosin family in mammalian tissue. It was originally isolated from calf thymus extract in 1981. Tβ4 functions primarily as the main intracellular G-actin sequestering peptide — it regulates the ratio of free actin monomers to polymerised actin filaments, which directly governs cell structure, motility, and division. It is found at highest concentrations in platelets, white blood cells, and at wound sites.

**History:** Thymosin Beta-4 research was pioneered by Allan Goldstein at George Washington University in the 1980s–1990s. RegeneRx Biopharmaceuticals (later rebranded) has been the primary driver of Tβ4 clinical trials, including trials for corneal wound healing, cardiac repair, and venous ulcers. TB-500 (the synthetic fragment) gained traction in the performance and biohacker communities in the 2010s, marketed largely as a more accessible and cost-effective alternative to full-length Tβ4.

**Important distinction:** TB-500 is NOT the same as full Tβ4. TB-500 refers specifically to the 17–23 fragment (LKKTETQ) or its acetylated form. A 2024 study found that TB-500's wound-healing activity may actually be mediated by its metabolite Ac-LKKTE rather than the parent Ac-LKKTETQ form.

**Classification:** Synthetic peptide / Actin-sequestering fragment / Growth factor modulator / Research chemical

## Mechanism of Action

TB-500's biological activity centres on actin dynamics and cellular migration:

### 1. G-Actin Sequestration & Cytoskeletal Regulation
TB-500 binds to monomeric G-actin (free actin), preventing it from polymerising into filamentous F-actin. This regulation of the G-actin:F-actin ratio is central to cellular motility. By freeing up G-actin availability, TB-500 allows cells to dynamically remodel their cytoskeleton — enabling rapid migration into damaged tissue zones.

### 2. Upregulation of Actin & Cell Migration
Beyond sequestration, TB-500 upregulates total actin expression and promotes the formation of ternary actin-profilin complexes. This drives the proliferation, migration, and differentiation of repair-critical cells: fibroblasts, keratinocytes, endothelial cells, and myoblasts.

### 3. Angiogenesis Promotion
TB-500 promotes endothelial cell migration and adhesion, stimulating the formation of new blood vessels (angiogenesis). The actin-binding domain has been specifically shown to be required for this angiogenic activity. In aged animals, angiogenesis is naturally reduced, and TB-500 restores this capacity. *Source: Philp et al., 2003, FASEB J — "The actin binding site on thymosin beta4 promotes angiogenesis."*

### 4. Extracellular Matrix (ECM) Remodelling
TB-500 promotes matrix metalloproteinase (MMP) expression during wound repair, enabling organised remodelling of the extracellular matrix. It also reduces myofibroblast activation, which is the key driver of scar tissue formation. The result is regeneration with less fibrosis.

### 5. NF-κB and MAPK Pathway Inhibition (Anti-inflammatory)
In vitro studies demonstrate TB-500 (and Tβ4) inhibit the NF-κB and MAPK signalling pathways, reducing production of pro-inflammatory mediators including inflammatory cytokines and chemokines. This directly modulates the acute and chronic inflammatory response.

### 6. Integrin-Linked Kinase (ILK) Activation
A key molecular event: TB-500/Tβ4 activates integrin-linked kinase (ILK), which in turn promotes cardiac cell migration, survival, and repair. This ILK activation pathway is considered essential for Tβ4's cardiac regenerative effects. *Source: Bock-Marquette et al., 2004, Nature.*

### 7. Metabolite Activity
A 2024 pharmacokinetic study (Rahaman et al., *J Chromatogr B*) found that TB-500's wound-healing activity in vitro may be primarily mediated by its metabolite **Ac-LKKTE** rather than the parent Ac-LKKTETQ form. This has implications for bioavailability and dosing, and suggests TB-500 acts partly as a prodrug.

## Primary Use Cases

### Clinical / Research Applications
- **Wound healing:** The most clinically developed application. Multiple clinical trials by RegeneRx tested RGN-137 (Tβ4 gel) for pressure ulcers and venous stasis ulcers (Phase II, positive outcomes). *Guarnera et al., 2010, Ann NY Acad Sci.*
- **Corneal wound healing:** RGN-259 (Tβ4 eye drops) — advanced Phase III trials for dry eye disease and neurotrophic keratopathy; shown to accelerate corneal re-epithelialization.
- **Cardiac repair:** Pilot clinical trial (RegeneRx, 2016) — Tβ4-treated endothelial progenitor cells transplanted into STEMI (heart attack) patients showed improved cardiac function. First human cardiac study with Tβ4.
- **Dentistry:** Emerging research on TB-500 for accelerating healing after extractions, periodontal surgery, and implant procedures.
- **Muscular dystrophy:** Animal data (Spurney et al., 2010) showed benefits in dystrophin-deficient mice.

### Biohacker / Performance Uses (Anecdotal + Preclinical Basis)
- **Muscle recovery:** Most common self-reported use; reduction in DOMS, faster recovery between training sessions
- **Tendon/ligament repair:** Particularly chronic or slow-healing injuries — rotator cuff, Achilles, hamstring, plantar fascia
- **Joint flexibility and mobility:** Users report improved range of motion, reduced stiffness
- **Scar tissue reduction:** Both prophylactic (preventing build-up) and therapeutic (breaking down existing scar tissue)
- **Hair loss / hair growth:** Preclinical data (Gao et al., 2015, *PLoS ONE*) shows Tβ4 induces hair follicle activation in mice; used anecdotally for androgenetic alopecia
- **Neuroprotection:** Animal data suggests TB-500 may support neuronal survival after TBI or stroke
- **Cardiovascular health:** Emerging interest based on cardiac regeneration data

**Overall evidence summary:** TB-500/Tβ4 has more advanced clinical evidence than BPC-157 in specific areas (corneal healing, venous ulcers, cardiac pilot study). However, for the most common biohacker uses (muscle/tendon/ligament recovery, flexibility), evidence remains preclinical. The distinction between full Tβ4 and the TB-500 fragment is often glossed over — most clinical trials used full-length Tβ4, not the isolated fragment sold as TB-500.

⚠️ **No large-scale human RCTs exist for musculoskeletal or general recovery applications of TB-500 specifically.**

## Risks & Contraindications

### Common Side Effects
- **Injection site reaction** — most common; mild redness, tenderness, induration
- **Mild fatigue or lethargy** — occasionally reported
- **Headache** — transient, mild
- **Temporary mood changes** — irritability, mild anxiety reported by some users
- **Dizziness / light-headedness** — rare

### Serious / Theoretical Concerns
- **Anaphylaxis risk:** At least one medical reference (DrOracle) cites documented hypersensitivity reactions including anaphylaxis, cardiovascular collapse, pulmonary oedema, and angioneurotic oedema — particularly with repeated administration. These are flagged as case-level reports. The source may reflect reactions to impure research-grade product. **This risk warrants caution, particularly in individuals with a history of peptide reactions or allergies.**
- **Cancer risk:** TB-500's pro-angiogenic and pro-proliferative mechanisms raise theoretical concern regarding tumour promotion. **Contraindicated in active malignancy.** This concern is theoretical and based on mechanism, not clinical observations.
- **Immune modulation:** TB-500 modulates immune responses; individuals with autoimmune conditions or on immunosuppressive therapy should consult a physician.
- **Unknown long-term safety:** No long-term human data exists. Cycling is recommended.

### Regulatory Status
- **FDA (USA):** TB-500 is classified as a **Category 2** bulk drug substance (alongside BPC-157). Compounding pharmacies cannot legally produce it for routine use as of 2023–2025. **Not FDA-approved for any indication.**
- **WADA:** TB-500 (Thymosin Beta-4 and its derivatives, including TB-500) is **explicitly listed on the WADA Prohibited List** under Section S2: Peptide Hormones, Growth Factors, Related Substances and Mimetics. Prohibited both in and out of competition. Any competitive athlete found using it faces disqualification.
- **Equine / veterinary use:** TB-500 was developed and widely used in veterinary medicine (particularly horse racing recovery). The equine doping control literature is more advanced than the human literature.
- **UK / EU / Australia:** Not approved; research chemical status; legal grey area for personal use varies by jurisdiction.

## Education Hook Facts

1. **It's in almost every cell in your body right now.** Thymosin Beta-4 (the parent of TB-500) is one of the most abundant proteins in mammalian cells — up to 10% of total intracellular protein in some cell types. Your body already makes it; TB-500 is an attempt to supercharge that system.

2. **Horses get it first.** TB-500 was initially developed and widely used in thoroughbred horse racing to accelerate recovery from muscle injuries. The veterinary literature on TB-500 is more established than the human literature — leading to its early detection methods being developed for equine anti-doping.

3. **It grew new heart tissue.** In a landmark 2004 Nature study, Thymosin Beta-4 was shown to activate dormant epicardial progenitor cells — effectively reactivating an embryonic cardiac repair programme that the adult heart is supposed to have "forgotten." It grew new coronary vessels in adult mice.

4. **It may explain why some wounds heal without scars.** TB-500 reduces the number of myofibroblasts in wounds — these are the cells responsible for scar tissue contraction and fibrosis. Less myofibroblast activation = more organised, lower-scar healing. This is why it's studied for anti-fibrotic applications.

5. **The active ingredient might be a metabolite, not the peptide itself.** A 2024 study found that TB-500's wound-healing activity is likely mediated by its breakdown product Ac-LKKTE, not the parent molecule. TB-500 may be acting as a prodrug in the body — a fact most users and practitioners don't know.

## Dosing Reference

*All dosing information is from preclinical literature, professional monographs, and practitioner protocols. No established human dosing for musculoskeletal use exists. This is for reference only.*

| Phase | Dose | Frequency | Duration |
|-------|------|-----------|----------|
| Loading / acute injury | 2.0–2.5 mg | 2–3x per week | Weeks 1–4 |
| Maintenance | 2.0–6.0 mg | 1–2x per month | After initial phase |
| General / wellness | 1.0–2.0 mg | 1–2x per week | 6–12 weeks |
| Typical weekly range | 4–8 mg total | Divided 2–3 administrations | — |

**Maximum cycle duration:** 3 months continuous dosing. If long-term use required: cycle 3 months on / 6 weeks off, or 6 weeks on / 6 weeks off (Professional Monograph, Scientific Performance Research).

**Administration route:** Subcutaneous (SubQ) injection is standard. Intramuscular (IM) also used. IV administration has been used in clinical cardiac trials with full Tβ4 under medical supervision.

**Reconstitution:** Lyophilised powder; reconstitute with bacteriostatic water. Store reconstituted peptide at 2–8°C, use within 30 days.
